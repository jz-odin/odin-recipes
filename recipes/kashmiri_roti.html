Kashmiri Rotis

Description

Kashmiri Roti is a lightly spiced flatbread from the Kashmir region of India, known for its subtle flavors and fragrant aroma. Traditionally made with whole wheat flour, it includes distinctive spices like fennel seeds and caraway (or ajwain), which give it a unique taste compared to regular rotis.

This roti is often enjoyed with noon chai (salted pink tea) or served alongside mild curries and vegetables. Soft, warm, and gently spiced, it brings a comforting touch to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1/4 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Warm water (as needed to knead)
  • Ghee or oil (for cooking)
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, fennel seeds, caraway seeds, salt, and sugar.
  2. Gradually add warm water and knead into a soft dough.
  3. Cover and rest the dough for 15â€“20 minutes.
  4. Divide into small balls, roll into flat rotis.
  5. Cook on a hot tawa/pan until both sides are golden, brushing with ghee if desired.
